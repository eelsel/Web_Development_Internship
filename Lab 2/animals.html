Amur Leopard

Panthera pardus orientalis 		Asia Temperate rain forests in the Russian Far East and parts of China Carnivore - deer, hares, small mammals Fewer than 120 are left in the wild, making it one of the rarest big cats on Earth.
Eurasian Lynx

Lynx lynx		 Europe (and parts of asia) Boreal and temperate forests The Eurasian lynx is the largest of the lynx species and is slowly returning to parts of Europe due to rewilding efforts.
Axolotl

Ambystoma mexicanum 		North America Freshwater lakes and canals (mainly Lake Xochimilco, Mexico) Carnivore - worms, insects, small fish Axolotls have the unique adaptation of regenerating entire limbs, parts of their heart, spinal cord, and even parts of their brain. This makes them a key subject in regeneration research.
Platypus

Ornithorhynchus anatinus 		Australia Freshwater rivers and streams The platypus is one of only mammals that lay eggs, and males are known to have venomous spurs on their hind.

Suricata suricatta		 Africa Arid savannas and deserts (Kalahari, Namib) Omnivore - insects, lizards, eggs, plants Meerkats live in complex social groups and take turns acting as sentinels, standing guard for predators.
